Bill Gates

Founder - Amazon

  • 1964

    Birth

    Jeff Bezos is born with the name Jeffrey Preston Jorgensen in Albuquerque, N.M., on Jan. 12. His mother, Jacklyn 'Jackie' Gise, divorces his father, Theodore Jorgensen, 18 months later and marries Miguel 'Mike' Bezos when Jeff is 4.

  • 1982

    A self-described 'goody,' Bezos graduates as class valedictorian from Miami Palmetto Senior High School. He tells The Miami Herald that he wants to build space hotels, amusement parks, yachts and colonies for 2 million or 3 million people orbiting the Earth.

  • 1986

    Bezos graduates from Princeton.

  • 1990

    Bezos joins N.Y. hedge fund

    Applying his computer savvy to stock trades, Bezos goes to work for D.E. Shaw, a quantitative hedge fund in New York. He quickly advances to become senior vice president.

  • 1993

    Bezos gets married

    At D.E. Shaw, Bezos meets his future wife, MacKenzie Tuttle, a Princeton graduate and former research assistant to novelist Toni Morrison. They marry at The Breakers resort in Palm Beach, Fla.

  • 1994

    Amazon is born

    Bezos leaves New York to start Amazon at the age of 30, setting up shop in a Bellevue rental house. He first calls Amazon 'Cadabra.com,' until discovering that it might sound like 'cadaver.com.'

  • 1995

    Amazon opens for business

    Amazon begins selling books on the Web in mid-July. Its first book sold is 'Fluid Concepts & Creative Analogies: Computer Models of the Fundamental Mechanisms of Thought.'

  • 1997

    Amazon goes public

    In one of the most talked-about technology deals of the year, Amazon goes public and begins selling shares at $18 each. A big winner is Bezos, whose 9.88 million shares are worth $290 million instantly.

  • 2000

    In September, Bezos creates a privately funded space venture called Blue Origin. By 2005, Bezos would begin construction on a rocket-ship complex in Kent, where Blue Origin would design and build spacecrafts.

  • 2003

    Bezos avoids serious injury in March when a helicopter he was riding in crashes on a mountain range in Texas.

  • 2011

    Market cap tops $100 billion

    With its stock above $222 a share in July, Amazon's total market capitalization breaks the $100 billion mark, making it only the second locally based company to do so.
