John Lennon

Singer.Writer.

  • October 9, 1940

    Birth

    Born in Liverpool to Julia Stanley and Alfred Lennon during a blitzkrieg attack.

  • 1956

    Julia buys John a mail-order guitar. He subsequently forms his first band, the Quarrymen (sometimes written as the Quarry Men).

  • 1957

    John asks Paul McCartney to join the Quarrymen while playing at a church fair in Liverpool.

  • 1958

    John writes "Hello Little Girl," his first song.

  • 1958

    George Harrison, a friend of Paul, joins the Quarrymen.

  • 1958

    John's mother is killed by an auto while crossing the road.

  • 1961

    The Beatles, with John Lennon as front man, debut at Liverpool's Cavern Club.

  • 1962

    John marries Cynthia Powell.

  • 1963

    Julian Lennon is born to John and Cynthia.

  • 1964

    John's first book, In His Own Write, is published..

  • 1965

    A Spaniard in the Works, John's second book, is published.

  • 1966

    John states that the Beatles are more popular than Jesus, setting off a backlash against the group and spurring the public burning of Beatle albums.

  • 1966

    John films How I Won the War, directed by Richard Lester, director of A Hard Day's Night. He writes "Strawberry Fields" during the filming.

  • 1966

    John meets Yoko Ono at a preview of her art show, Exhibition #2, at London's Indica Gallery.

  • 1968

    John moves out of his home in Weybridge and moves into Ringo's apartment in Montague Square with Yoko Ono.

  • 1968

    John and Cynthia are divorced.

  • 1969

    John and Yoko are married on Gibraltar.

  • 1970

    John debuts his first album, Plastic Ono Band.

  • 2009

    Senator Strom Thurmond tells Attorney General John Mitchell that John should be deported because he consorts with known radicals such as Jerry Rubin and Abbie Hoffman.

  • 1972

    John begins a four-year fight against deportation.

  • 1974

    John makes his final concert appearance at Madison Square Garden with Elton John.

  • 1980

    John Lennon is assassinated outside the Dakota.

  • 1984

    John is inducted into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as a solo artist.
